About

Assistant Inspector General of Police Musa Yusuf Garba (Rtd) is a distinguished teacher, public administrator, criminologist, forensic psychologist, strategic security expert, certified security investigator, community policing specialist, and mobile police commander. CITATION FOR AIG MUSA YUSUF GARBA psc+, IIPS, SPSP. AIG Musa Yusuf Garba a Teacher, a Public Administrator, a Criminologist and Forensic Psychologist, a Strategic Security expert, a Certified Security Investigator, an instructor, a mentor, a community policing specialist, a distinguished mobile police commander and a Supercop, who has been providing leadership and mentoring his subordinates to achieving results. He was born in Gusau Local Government Area of Zamfara State on 1 January, to the family a philanthropist and an industrialist Alhaji Garba Maigoro Gusau and Hajiya Fatima Nee (Matar Mallam) all of Gusau Local Government Area of Zamfara State, Nigeria. He started Quranic education in Gusau before he was in 1973 enrolled at Township Primary School Gusau and later Nizamiyya Islamiya Primary School, Gusau, where he obtained his First School Leaving Certificate in 1979. The young Musa then, got admission into Government Secondary School Bungudu and obtained his GCE O/level Certificate with flying colours in 1984. AIG Musa got admitted into the then University of Sokoto, now Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto, and obtained a bachelor's degree in Education Islamic Studies in 1989. He was posted to the then Bendel State and now Edo State for his National Youth Service where he was deployed to Alhaja Fadila Primary and Secondary Schools in Odemwingie in Benin City. After completing his National Youth Service in 1991, he started his career with the then Sokoto State Ministry of Education, where he was deployed to Giginya Memorial Secondary School, Sokoto as Master II. However, AIG Musa's passion for uniform service led him to withdraw his services from the Sokoto State Ministry of Education and was enlisted into the Nigeria Police Academy Annex, Kaduna as a Cadet Assistant Superintendent of Police on the 18th May 1992.

AIG Musa held several operational, investigative, and administrative positions in the Force since his passing out from the Police Academy and being commissioned as an Assistant Superintendent of Police. As usual for the newly passed-out cadet officers, he was posted to Lafia Division of the then Plateau State and now Nasarawa State for his one-year attachment. On successful completion of the attachment, he was posted to Shandam Division as Admin Officer; that was where he learnt and mastered the rudiments of police administrative jobs. AIG Musa also served in Taraba State as the Divisional Crime Officer in Gembu, Officer in Charge (O/C) at Ngroje and Tella Out-stations as well as the Commanding Officer of Police Mobile Force 40 Squadron, Jalingo. In 1999, AIG Garba became the first Aid De Camp (ADC) to the former Governor of Zamfara State Alh. Ahmed Sani Yariman Bakura till 2003, after his promotion to the rank of Superintendent of Police, a rank above what is obtainable for the rank of ADC, he was posted to Zamfara State C.I.D. as 2ic to AC CID. The ethical AIG later served as O/C General Investigation (G.I.)in C.I.D Kaduna Command. AIG Garba was at Police College Kaduna from 2004 to 2007 as O/C Computer. After the mandatory three-year service at the Police College, he was nominated to attend the Community Policing Course at Houston, Texas, United States of America (USA). On his successful completion of the course, Musa Garba returned to the Police College, and he was later posted to Sokoto State Command, where he served as Divisional Police Officer (DPO) at Unguwar Rogo Division. As a trained Community Policing Officer, AIG Musa served as Coordinator of Community Policing in Katsina, FCT, and Kaduna State Commands, respectively. He also served as Divisional Police Officer at Mpape Division of the FCT Abuja, Sabon Gari as well as Kurmin Mashi Divisions of Kaduna State. As CSP Garba was again deployed as the Commanding Officer Police Mobile Force 41 Squadron Damaturu, Yobe State, from 2011 to 2014. On his promotion to the rank of Assistant Commissioner of Police, AIG Musa was posted as Assistant Commissioner of Police in charge of operations in Kebbi State, from where he was posted to Rivers State as Area Commander Bori, Ogoni Land. In 2018,

AIG Garba was promoted to the rank of Deputy Commissioner of Police and subsequently redeployed to the Force Headquarters Abuja, as DC Armaments. Three months later, he was posted to Police Training School Ikeja, Lagos State, as the Commandant, where he made an indelible mark that will be remembered for a long time. On the 28th March 2023, DC Musa was promoted to the enviable rank of Commissioner of Police and subsequently deployed to the strategic Kaduna State Command as the 41st CP of the state and assumed duty on the 2nd May 2023. He was thereafter redeployed to the Police Premier Institution, the Police Staff College Jos, as the Deputy Commandant. In 2025, he was promoted to the exalted rank of an Assistant Inspector General of Police (AIG) and was subsequently deployed to Lagos as the AIG (Maritime Command). In August this year, AIG Musa was redeployed to Force CID Annex Kaduna, from where he meritoriously retired from the service of the Nigerian police. There is no doubt that AIG Musa Yusuf Garba will be greatly missed by the Nigerian Police community and Nigeria at large. AIG Musa was a well-trained police officer who has attended various professional police training programs. Some of which include, Police Mobile Training at Police Mobile Force Training College, Gwoza (1993), Intermediate Command Course at Police Staff College, Jos (2007), Strategic Leadership Command Course at Police Staff College, Jos (2018), Community Policing Course at Houston Texas, USA (2007). He also obtaobtained a Certificate in Enterprise Security Risk Management (2020), Certificate in Kidnapping and Hostage Survival (2021), Certificate on Protecting Expatriates in Hostile Environment (2021), Certified Safety/Protector Supervisor (2021), Certificate on Leading Organization out of Security Crises (2020), Certified Security Investigator Course (2021) and Executive Diploma in Private Detective Tradecraft (2021). AIG Garba's thirst for knowledge made him to further pursue various academic programs in order to enhance his capacity for effective discharge of his duties. He holds two Master degrees in Criminology and Forensic Psychology, and a Masters in Philosophy (M.Phil.), both from the Nigerian Defence Academy (NDA) Kaduna in 2021 and 2023 respectively, Post graduate Diploma in Public Administration at the Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto (2016), Diploma in Law, Kaduna Polytechnic (2006), Advance Diploma in Strategic Security and infrastructure Protection, University of Lagos (UNILAG) (2021) and Executive Diploma in Private Detective Tradecraft (Elizade University llara Mokin, Ondo State (2022). AIG Musa is currently a PhD. student in Leadership and Strategic Studies at the Nigerian Defense Academy, Kaduna. He is affiliated to many professional bodies and organizations, which include: a Fellow, International Institute of Professional Security (IIPS), Fellow, Institute of Security Nigeria (ISN), Fellow, Institute of Criminological Studies, Fellow, Security Management (ICSSM) and Fellow, Institute of Strategic Public Negotiations (ISPN). AIG Musa's outstanding public relations and community development have earned him recognition by the Gusau Emirate Council, with the title of 'Gado da Masu'of Gusau by His Royal Highness, the late Emir Alhaji (Dr.) Ibrahim Bello. AIG Musa Yusuf Garba is widely travelled, amongst the countries he visited were: the United States of America, the United Kingdom, France, Italy, Germany, Spain, India, China, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, the United Arab Emirates, South Africa, Benin Republic, Sudan, Ethiopia, Senegal, Cameroon, Mali, Egypt, and the Niger Republic, amonamongst others.
